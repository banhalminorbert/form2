Frequently Asked

Questions we are glad to answer openly

Why does G.O.A.T not publish client names?
Client confidentiality is a core part of the service. Publishing names, references or assignment details would compromise the privacy and security our clients rely on. Relevant credentials can be reviewed confidentially after a qualified inquiry.
Are credentials available?
Yes. Founder credentials, training background and selected, appropriately redacted references are available during confidential qualification, under a non-disclosure agreement where required.
Does G.O.A.T work with family offices?
Yes. We provide protective liaison for family offices and private offices, with awareness of household rhythm, family schedule continuity and the privacy of family members.
Does G.O.A.T provide general guarding?
No. We are a specialist executive and close protection partner, not a general manned-guarding provider.
Does G.O.A.T work internationally?
Yes, with a focus on movement between Europe and the Gulf. Services are provided subject to applicable local law, licensing, insurance and contractual conditions.
How is personnel vetted?
We work through dedicated, vetted protective cells with documented due diligence. Detailed vetting standards are reviewed confidentially during qualification.
Why is the team not publicly shown?
Operational discretion requires that personnel are not publicly identifiable. This protects both our clients and our team.
How does the confidential inquiry process work?
Inquiry begins with initial confidential contact, followed by scope review, a compliance and feasibility check, private credential review where appropriate, and contract-bound protective planning. Sensitive itinerary, identity or threat details should not be included in the first message.
